# NYC Property Management Fee Structure Reference

> Source: Compiled from Brick Underground, Cooperator News, FirstService Residential, PropertyClub NYC,
> Hudson Property Services, Habitat Magazine, Hauseit, Yoreevo, and NY AG Tenants' Rights Guide.
> Reflects typical fees as of 2023–2025.

## Base Management Fees by Asset Class

### Co-op/Condo (Large, Full-Service)
- **Manhattan (High-End):** ~$1,000+ per unit/year (luxury doorman; large firms may require $50K–$100K min annual)
- **Outer Boroughs (Bronx, Brooklyn, Queens):** ~$500–$800 per unit/year
- **Riverdale / S. Westchester:** ~$400–$600 per unit/year

### Co-op/Condo (Small/Mid-size, Walk-up)
- **Manhattan:** ~$600–$800 per unit/year (smaller management firm)
- **Outer Boroughs:** ~$300–$500 per unit/year
- **Riverdale / S. Westchester:** ~$300–$500 per unit/year

### Rental Building (Multifamily)
- **Manhattan:** ~5%–8% of annual rent roll (large luxury); ~8%–12% of monthly rent (small landlords)
- **Outer Boroughs:** ~8%–12% of monthly rent; some firms $150–$300 per unit/month flat
- **Riverdale / S. Westchester:** ~6%–10% of monthly rent; flat per-unit fees ~$150/mo for small multi-families

### Individual Rental Units (1–4 units)
- **Manhattan:** ~10% of monthly rent
- **Outer Boroughs:** ~10%–12% of rent
- **Westchester Suburbs:** ~8%–10% of rent

## Ancillary Fees ("Schedule A" Charges)

| Fee / Service | Typical Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding / Setup | $250–$500 (one-time) | Initial account setup, data entry, inspections |
| Leasing New Tenant | 50%–100% of one month's rent | Tenant placement / leasing commission |
| Lease Renewal | $150–$300 flat fee | Per renewal |
| Vacancy Fee | Varies ($0–$50 flat) | Some charge nothing; others reduced flat fee |
| Maintenance Coordination | 10%–20% markup on vendor invoices or $5–$10/work order | |
| Project Management (Capital) | 2%–15% of project cost | For large construction/renovation oversight |
| Eviction Processing | $200–$500 per case (plus legal) | |
| Early Termination | $100–$500 (one-time) | Owner cancels contract early |
| Refinancing (Co-op Underlying) | Negotiated flat fee (thousands) | |
| Tax Abatement Filing | Varies | J-51, 421-a, co-op/condo abatement |
| Required Compliance Filings | Varies | Fire safety, lead paint, window guards |
| Annual Financial Statements | Usually included | Form 1098 may incur small fee |
| Additional Board Meetings | Hourly or flat fee | Beyond contracted monthly meeting |
| Sale/Transfer Application | ~$500 processing fee (buyer pays) | Credit checks, board approval |
| Move-In / Move-Out Fee | ~$500 each (non-refundable) | Plus $500–$1,000 refundable deposit |
| Financing (Co-op Buyer Loan) | $250–$500 (buyer pays) | Recognition agreement fee |
| Sublet Application | ~$100–$250 processing fee | |
| Sublet Fee (Co-op) | 20%–30% of monthly maintenance | Monthly surcharge to shareholder |
| Rental Application Fee | $50–$150 per applicant | Co-ops exempt from $20 cap |
| Board Processing Fee (rentals) | $500–$1,500 (tenant pays) | Manhattan higher; outer boroughs lower |
| Move-In Deposit (renting) | $500–$1,000 (refundable) | |
| Pet Fees | Varies by building | Not universal |
| Late Payment Fees | Up to $50 or 5% of rent | NY State law caps |

## Key Principles

1. Higher base fee often = more services included (no nickel-and-diming)
2. Lower base + à la carte may benefit small buildings handling some tasks in-house
3. Compare TOTAL package, not just base fee percentage
4. Large firms often impose $50K+ annual minimums — unsuitable for small buildings
5. Buildings pay roughly $500–$1,200/apartment/year in management fees on average
