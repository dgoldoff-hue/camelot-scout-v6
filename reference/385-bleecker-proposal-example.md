# Camelot Proposal of Services — Reference Templates
## Source: 385 Bleecker St (rental) + 415 E 6th St (condo)

## Structure (BOTH follow this exactly):
1. Date
2. Att: [Contact Name] / C/O [Entity]
3. "Dear [First Name],"
4. Opening: "It was a pleasure speaking with you..." / "Thank you for your time..."
5. **The Property:** address, block & lot, client name, unit mix, payroll, management scope
6. **Team:** 1 PM, 1 Admin, In-House CPA/Bookkeeper
7. **Scope bullets:** (varies by property type — see notes below)
8. **Fee:** Single annual number based on "comparable buildings we manage, unit count, and expected time"
9. **"Terms spelled out in our standard [type] Management Agreement (attached/separate cover)"**
10. Website + direct contact info (office x701, cell, email)
11. "Once again, thank you... we look forward to working with you soon."
12. **Respectfully yours, David A. Goldoff, President**
13. **Next Steps section:** "Within the first 60-days..."
14. **Transition narrative** (30-60 days prose)
15. **Budget/Facility/Staff Review** (prose)
16. **Meet & Greet** (Wine & Cheese, lobby/roof deck, Zoom option)

## Key language patterns:
- "We are confident we can help"
- "These services will include but not limited to"
- "We will immediately perform a site inspection"
- "keeping the building clean, safe, compliant"
- "circumvent those unwarranted stresses"
- "putting a face behind an email"

## Condo-specific adaptations (415 E 6th):
- Client = "The Board of Managers"
- Common charges (not rent)
- "Condo Management Agreement"
- Condo abatement submissions
- Board meetings "on as-needed basis"
- "Association" language

## Rental-specific adaptations (385 Bleecker):
- Client = owner/landlord name
- Rent (not common charges)
- "Management Agreement"
- Lease renewals, credit checks
- "Keep units fully leased at all times"
- DHCR/rent stabilized language if applicable

## Fee format:
- Simple flat annual number: "$10,200.00 dollars annually" or "$12,000.00 dollars annually"
- NOT per-unit pricing, NOT tiered
- "paid out in monthly installments"
- "based on your budget and comparable managed buildings"

## IMPORTANT: David uses a FLAT ANNUAL FEE, not per-unit tiers
